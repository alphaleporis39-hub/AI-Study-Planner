Features & How-To

Add your subjects with estimated study hours and priorities. The AI automatically generates a balanced weekly timetable distributing subjects across your available days and hours per day, giving more time to high-priority subjects.
The Pomodoro technique splits study time into 25-minute focused blocks followed by 5-minute breaks. This reduces mental fatigue and improves concentration. In StudyHub AI, you can fully customise work duration, break duration, and number of cycles.
Go to the Progress Tracker page. Select a subject, enter minutes studied, and click Log. The tracker shows a progress bar for each subject and an overall completion percentage based on your estimated hours.
On the Exam Countdown page, add your exam name and date. A real-time countdown will display days, hours, minutes, and seconds remaining. Upcoming exams also appear on your dashboard so you never lose sight of them.
Yes! The Timetable Editor lets you add, modify, or remove individual time slots for any day. You can set custom start and end times and assign any subject to each slot.
Yes. On the Timetable page, click Export PDF to download your weekly schedule as a printable PDF document.
The AI Tips section provides personalised study advice based on proven learning science — techniques like spaced repetition, active recall, interleaving, and more. Click "Refresh Tips" to get a new set of recommendations.
StudyHub AI can send you browser notifications reminding you to drink water at set intervals (45, 60, or 90 minutes). Enable it in Settings → Hydration Reminder.

Privacy & Data

Your study data is stored locally in your browser's localStorage — it never leaves your device. We use Google Analytics for anonymous usage statistics only. We do not sell or share your personal information. See our Privacy Policy.
Since data is stored in localStorage, clearing your browser cache or storage will delete your StudyHub AI data. We recommend noting down important schedule information before clearing browser data.
StudyHub AI uses localStorage (not traditional cookies) for your study data. Google Analytics may set cookies for anonymous analytics. See our Privacy Policy for full details.
